How to Use INTRA

INTRA is designed for speed and precision. This guide covers effective querying, common use cases, and integration strategies to help you retrieve operational knowledge without disrupting workflow momentum.

Getting Started

Access Instructions

INTRA is accessible to authorized NSW advisors and staff through a secure login. If you haven't received access credentials, contact your team administrator.

Basic Query Structure

INTRA works best with specific, contextual questions:

Effective queries:

  • "What are National Life Group's current underwriting requirements for life insurance applications?"
  • "What is the minimum account threshold for Howard Capital Management portfolios?"
  • "What are the 2025 FINRA cybersecurity compliance priorities?"

Less effective queries:

  • "Tell me about insurance"
  • "What's new?"
  • "Compliance stuff"
Key principle: Be specific. Reference carriers, products, or regulatory bodies when relevant.

Common Use Cases

Underwriting Requirements

Retrieve current medical exam requirements, build charts, and risk assessment criteria for specific carriers. Useful during case design and pre-underwriting phases.

Example query: "What are OneAmerica's APS requirements for LTC applications?"

Account Minimums & Thresholds

Access minimum investment thresholds for third-party money managers, clearing firms, and specialized account types.

Example query: "What is the minimum for opening a non-qualified account with Cambridge Investment Research?"

Compliance Standards

Query 2025 regulatory updates from FINRA, SEC, and DOL. Understand current examination priorities, fiduciary obligations, and cybersecurity mandates.

Example query: "What are the SEC's 2025 examination priorities for RIAs?"

Carrier Information

Retrieve carrier-specific application procedures, contracting requirements, signature protocols, and commission schedules.

Example query: "Does New York Life accept DocuSign applications or require hand-signed paper contracts?"

Product Specifications

Access detailed product guides, including LTC solutions, annuities, and investment structures. Understand features, underwriting, and funding options.

Example query: "What is the difference between AssetCare and AnnuityCare from OneAmerica?"

Operations Procedures

Query standard operating procedures for case design, new business workflows, application tracking, and policy delivery processes.

Example query: "What is the timeline for the APS process in new business underwriting?"

Query Best Practices

Frame Questions with Context

The more context you provide, the more precise the answer.

Good:

"What are the delivery procedures for approved life insurance policies?"

Better:

"What are the delivery procedures for approved life insurance policies, including required forms and placement confirmation steps?"

Use Proper Names and Terminology

Reference specific entities when applicable:

  • Carriers: "Ameritas," "National Life Group," "OneAmerica"
  • Regulations: "Reg BI," "PTE 2020-02," "FINRA Rule 2165"
  • Tools: "Redtail CRM," "HCM-BuyLine," "SuranceBay"

Ask Follow-Up Questions

If the initial response is too broad, narrow the scope:

  1. "What are FINRA's cybersecurity priorities?"
  2. (Review response, then follow up): "What specific controls does FINRA recommend for ransomware prevention?"

Integrating INTRA into Daily Workflows

INTRA is designed to eliminate context-switching. Rather than pausing work to search documentation or draft emails, query INTRA in the moment.

Integration Points

During Case Design:

  • Query pre-underwriting requirements before running illustrations
  • Confirm carrier-specific application procedures
  • Verify product specifications and underwriting criteria

During New Business Processing:

  • Check application submission timelines
  • Retrieve APS ordering procedures
  • Confirm delivery document requirements

During Compliance Reviews:

  • Access current regulatory standards
  • Verify fiduciary obligation frameworks
  • Review conflict of interest disclosure requirements

During Client Meetings:

  • Query product features for real-time answers
  • Confirm account minimums and thresholds
  • Retrieve tax treatment or planning considerations

Tips & Troubleshooting

If you receive a generic or incomplete answer:

  1. Rephrase with more specificity
  2. Add relevant context (carrier name, product type, regulatory body)
  3. Break complex questions into smaller, focused queries

If information seems outdated:

INTRA is updated regularly, but if you suspect a discrepancy, verify with the source carrier or regulatory body. Report outdated information to your administrator for manual review.

When to escalate instead of querying:

  • Unique client situations requiring judgment
  • Complex compliance interpretations
  • Negotiations or relationship-specific details
INTRA excels at retrieving documented procedures and standards. For nuanced decision-making, consult your team or compliance officer.
